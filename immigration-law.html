Our practice covers the full arc of Canadian immigration: economic, family, humanitarian, and protected-person streams. Whether you are applying from abroad, adjusting status in Canada, or appealing an unfavourable decision, we build a strategy around your circumstances and your timeline.

FAQ

Immigration questions, answered.

Do I need a lawyer for an immigration application?

Straightforward applications can be filed without legal help. But most immigration applications involve nuance — eligibility, supporting evidence, credibility, and procedural rules. A single poorly-answered question can result in refusal or even misrepresentation findings. A lawyer's role is to identify the strongest pathway, prepare the file properly, and respond strategically if issues arise.

How long does Express Entry take?

From invitation to apply (ITA) to permanent residence, most Express Entry applications are processed within six months once submitted. Time before the ITA — improving your CRS score, language tests, credential assessment — varies widely based on your profile.

What is a spousal sponsorship and which stream applies?

If you are a Canadian citizen or PR and your spouse or common-law partner wishes to immigrate, you may sponsor them. Inland sponsorship is for spouses already in Canada (offering an open work permit during processing); outland sponsorship is typically for partners outside Canada. Each has different advantages; we help you choose based on your circumstances.

My application was refused. Can I appeal?

It depends on the decision. Sponsorship refusals, removal orders, and residency obligation decisions can be appealed to the Immigration Appeal Division. Refusals of visa applications abroad generally cannot be appealed but may be challenged by judicial review at the Federal Court. Timelines for both are strict — contact us as soon as you receive a refusal.

What is a humanitarian and compassionate (H&C) application?

An H&C application asks the Minister to grant permanent residence on humanitarian grounds when an applicant does not qualify under other streams. The assessment considers establishment in Canada, the best interests of any affected children, and hardship if the applicant had to leave. H&C is discretionary and requires a carefully prepared file.

Can I work while my PR application is processed?

In many cases, yes — through a valid work permit or, for inland spousal sponsorship applicants, through an open work permit available while the application is processed. We help you maintain lawful status throughout your immigration process.
